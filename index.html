I am exploring the possibility of entering the embedded systems development field, so I decided to practice my C programming skills. My knowledge of C is fairly limited, as I only learned the basics during my undergraduate studies a few years ago. Because of that, I’m not ashamed to appear like a beginner while reviewing it. Today, I attempted to solve a problem from LeetCode using C.

The problem: https://leetcode.com/problems/longest-common-prefix/description/.

For now, my view on how to solve a problem like LeetCode is: first, you come up with a logical sequence of steps; second, try to translate your logic into the programming language in your mind roughly; third, code while considering potential bugs in your code. The key point is—coding should be the last step.

Copilot summarized my logic very clearly (I relied heavily on Copilot this time since I found myself forgetting most of the syntax of the language):

  1. Find the shortest word in the strs array.
  2. Use the shortest word as a reference to compare characters with other words literally.
  3. If a mismatch is found, return the common prefix up to that point.
  4. If no mismatch is found, return the entire shortest word.

The shortest word is required here to avoid the index being out of bounds.

Ottawa Arrived

Hi,

It’s been a long time since my last post… There are some drafts (actually, only one) that I wrote during this time, but I’ll never finish them since I just deleted them. As the title suggests, yes, I’m finally here.

For the outbound journey, I put considerable effort into creating a backup plan in case any unexpected issues arose at customs, such as them not recognizing my student status after being on leave for so long. I kept this change very quiet, only informing my leader. I also managed to secure approval from HR to retain my position until I entered Canada. I worked up until the day before my flight, ensuring that if the worst-case scenario occurred at customs, I could still return and continue working. No celebrations, no vacation.

However, nothing happened. The CBSA didn’t even ask a single question. Two years of delay, and nobody cared. I don’t know if I should be happy about how smooth it went or feel something else entirely.

Now it’s been three weeks since then. Sometimes I sit near the window, looking out at the city—which isn’t exactly fancy but different from my hometown—and still wonder if I’m in a dream. Honestly, it’s good that I’m here. If I’d stayed in that position, I would have wasted my youth and probably burned out within a few years. But Ottawa—it’s just not exactly what I imagined.

I find that there’s nowhere to go in this city. It’s weird because I’m someone who is naturally curious and loves exploring new places. Before this, whenever I arrived in a city like Beijing or Chongqing, I was fully interested in finding a destination, pinning it on the map, and walking there just to see what it looked like. I’m not saying I’ve already lost my curiosity about Ottawa—no, that’s not it. Like I said, it does feel different from anywhere I’ve been before in China. But it feels like fruit that’s been frozen in the fridge. It’s still fresh, and it stays that way, but you just can’t taste the freshness.

Maybe I’m blaming the snow here. There’s snow everywhere. I’m from a northern city in China, and in my hometown, snow is something you encounter once or twice a year, maybe a few times, but it’s rare enough to feel lovely. Here in Ottawa, I’ve seen more snow in a single week than I did in my entire life before this. Though I still enjoy grabbing handfuls of snow and walking through untouched snowfields, I’ve noticed that a lot of people here don’t care about such things. And snow sticks to everything. Any color covered in white eventually turns grey. The red of bricks, the black of cars, the green of trees… all grey. Through my window, Ottawa becomes a grey city. It can be a bit depressing, isn’t it?

I feel a little isolated, and I know my language skills are weak. It’s good that I’m aware of what I need to do. I’ve only enrolled in one course this semester because I’m afraid my English won’t be able to keep up, but for this course, I’m doing well so far. This master’s program is still a challenge due to the tight time limits, but I’ll keep going.

This blog has turned into a diary, so let it be.

Brief Introduction

Hi there, my name is Yunze Lu (陆 云泽), and I am from China. I consider this my personal website, and I currently deploy it via GitHub Pages. As you can see, it was first built in July ‘23 but never used by me. Today, I’ve been organizing and exploring it, and I intend to start updating some content on it later. This post is to document that decision, so it’s an introduction or a prologue.

Just in case you’re really interested, I’m now a month away from my 24th birthday. Two years ago, I completed my undergraduate degree in Software Engineering from Yunnan University. At that time, I applied to the Master’s program in Electrical and Computer Engineering at the University of Ottawa, but to this day, I still haven’t received my study permit. So, I guess I’m kind of in graduate flunking status. In the meantime, I got myself an internship in China through the University of Ottawa’s co-op program, doing some Java development for an insurance company. This year, I managed to make the switch. Despite many other thoughts, I suspect I’ll be spending some time with this company in the future. All in all, I consider myself an IT practitioner.

Like many IT practitioners, I will have a blog of my own (this one). I’ll put it on my resume, probably. Aside from that, blogs are of practical use because after working, I realized that you can’t really remember everything. If you do something to a certain point, then pause for a while, and go back to it, you will have no clue about it, and recalling it can be very painful (like organizing this site today). So, writing things down is necessary. In fact, I haven’t quite figured out what type of content I’ll be posting on it, whether it’ll be completely technical or more like a diary. Certainly, I should keep this self-talking style rather than making it very formal because I don’t want it to become hard to stick to. Also, I’ll try to write in English as much as possible to work on my English writing skills.

Well, what can I say? See you in the next post.
